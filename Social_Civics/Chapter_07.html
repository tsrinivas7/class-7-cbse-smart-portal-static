## MEDIA
Media refers to any kind of means used to convey information. Mass media refers to the type of media designed to reach or communicate with a large number of people at a time.

The various types of mass media are:
- Television (cable network, satellite, etc.)
- Radio
- Cinema
- Print media (newspapers, magazines, journals, etc.)
- Internet

## Right to Information Act

Right to Information means any material in any form including records, documents, memos, e-mails, opinions, advices, press releases, circulars, orders, contracts, reports, papers, samples, models or data material held in any electronic or print form and information related to any private body which can be accessed by a public, as per the conditions of Law. Any citizen of India may request to obtain information from a public authority.

Right to Information Act (RTI) came into force on the 12 October, 2005. The Act extended to whole of India except the state of Jammu and Kashmir. After lifting up of Article 370 of the Indian Constitution, Jammu and Kashmir was also included under this act.

### Right to information includes the right to:

- inspect work, documents, records.
- take notes, extracts or certified copies of documents or records.

## Advertising

Advertising is the name given to the process of commercial promotion of goods and services in order to increase the sales. Advertising can be done by means of a number of media like television, newspapers, wall paintings, billboards, magazines, internet and by the word-of-mouth. Advertising informs the buyers about the availability of a certain product or service in the market and encourages them to buy it.

The advertisements are designed to influence a class of people. So, we see different types of advertisements meant to target the viewers/users of varying age groups such as infants, children, girls, young, middle aged and senior citizens.

### Social Media Advertising

Advertising on social media sites is definitely effective. Social media is open and transparent to its audience, so it makes sense for brands to be there on social media sites. The social media helps us in spreading information among the masses.

Advertising is the best way to influence public opinion by means of communicating with the masses again and again. Manufacturers and producers grab the attention of their customers by advertising their product's quality, durability and usefulness of the things in our day-to-day life.

Social Media Marketing (SMM) is the next big thing in Advertising. SMM is a form of internet or online marketing. It aims to achieve its branding and marketing goals through various social media networks, social web applications and through various other sources like 3D worlds such as Second Life, Active Worlds, etc.

## Why Advertising?

Advertising is the promotion of a company's products and services carried out primarily to drive sales of the products and services and also to build a brand identity and communicate changes or launching of new products/services to the customers. Advertising has become an essential element of the corporate world and hence the companies allot a considerable amount of revenues to their advertising budget. There are several reasons for advertising. These are as follows:

- to increase the sales of the product/service.
- to create and maintain a brand identity or brand image.

## Advertising: Advantages and Disadvantages

Advertising has a number of advantages. Apart from promoting commercial goods, advertising can also be used to educate and motivate the public about non-commercial issues such as AIDS, water and electricity conservation, deforestation, family planning, etc. It is a powerful media which is capable of reaching out to the masses living in remote areas. Now-a-days we find many advertisements on the internet also. These advertisements in most cases, have been successfully connecting the users with the information they require.

# Consumerism

In today's world, we are highly affected by advertisements. People are brand conscious and strive to maintain a particular standard. We can also say that we live in a materialistic world and this materialisation has affected our values as well. Possessing material goods, physical comfort and luxury have become more important things than the coordination between the mind, body and soul. This immense craving and inclination towards the material things is referred to as consumerism.

## Media and Ethics

Media plays an important role in a democratic country. It is the main responsibility of the media to decide which news is worthy of attention of the people and which is not. Some of the basic principles of media ethics are listed below:

(i) It should provide us true reports only.

(ii) It should act independently and should not be biased.

(iii) It should minimise the harm done to the society.

## Media and Democracy

As people need food, shelter and health care for their survival, they need communication for their social welfare. Moreover, for human dignity, people need certain traits that are intrinsic to genuine democracy; reason, responsibility, mutual respect, freedom of expression, and freedom of conscience, all of which are provided by means of mass media through communication.

A prerequisite of democracy, therefore, is the democratisation of communication. The media facilitates this process by providing an arena for public debate and by reconstituting private citizens as a public body in the form of public opinion. It is also necessary to make public communication an integral part of democracy. People should have full access to the knowledge and information they require. They should be able to discuss matters of public interest in order to influence the action taken.

A few years ago, the press was the main mass media channel, which reported on political conditions prevailing in the country. But today, political information is dominant on the national television channel as well as satellite channels. All these media channels compete with one another to bring people closer to the political process, important issues linked with them and their possible effects on society.

The phenomenal growth in mass media channels has led to an expansion of the role it could play in various areas apart from providing information. Such as influencing public opinion and social attitudes, entertainment and bringing about greater social integration.
