Lesson 9: Cyber Threats and Security

Introduction

Computers and the internet are essential parts of modern life, used for banking, shopping, communication, and entertainment. While they offer great opportunities, they also come with challenges known as cyber threats or cybercrimes. These threats can affect individuals, property, and society.

CYBERCRIME

A cybercrime is any illegal activity done through the internet. It falls under the jurisdiction of laws like the Indian Penal Code (IPC).
* Cybercrime — Any illegal activity done through the internet, such as identity theft, credit card theft, fraud, forgery, or harassment.
* Indian Penal Code (IPC) — The legal framework in India that covers various cybercrimes like sending threatening messages or forging documents.
* Cybercrimes are divided into three main categories: crimes against an individual person, crimes against property, and crimes against an organisation or society.
* Examples include stealing email IDs, fake lottery messages, ordering goods in someone else's name, and extracting mobile contacts.
* Hacking — Cracking into someone else's computer to steal information without their permission.

CYBERCRIMES AGAINST AN INDIVIDUAL PERSON

These offences cause harm or threat specifically to an individual user.

E-MAIL SPOOFING

In this crime, the receiver is fooled into believing a message comes from a trusted source when it does not.
* Spoofing — Means hoax or misleading; making a receiver believe a message is from a trusted source when it is actually bogus.
* When a user clicks on a falsely provided link in a spoofed email, malware or virus enters their system.
* The sender hides their true identity to make the email appear legitimate.

CYBERSTALKING

This involves harassing a victim through digital means.
* Digital Footprints — Traces left behind by a person's activities online.
* A stalker harasses a victim through emails, social media, chat rooms, blogs, or instant messaging.
* Stalkers may post false information to humiliate the victim, monitor online activities, or send viruses to damage the victim's computer.
* A stalker can be an online stranger or someone known to the targeted person.

CYBERBULLYING

This is the deliberate act of harming or harassing someone using information technology.
* Cyberbullying — An act of harming or harassing through information technology in a deliberate manner.
* It includes sending insulting remarks, threatening messages, spreading rumors, or posting embarrassing photos and videos.
* Remarks may be derogatory against gender, race, religion, or nationality.
* Defamation — Defined as a communication that intends to harm or damage the reputation of a person, business, product, government, religion, or nation.
* Malware — Any malicious program on a computer or mobile device, such as virus, worm, Trojan horse, Spyware, etc.

CYBERCRIMES AGAINST PROPERTY

Criminals steal or rob digital property just as they would physical property in real life.

IDENTITY THEFT

This refers to stealing and misusing someone's personal information.
* Identity Theft — Refers to the act of stealing and misuse of someone's personal information.
* Stolen information includes names, dates of birth, account numbers, credit/debit card numbers, and ATM pins.
* Hackers steal this information to obtain genuine documents, new credit cards, withdraw money, or get passports in the victim's name.
* Keylogger Programs — Used to check the user's keystrokes and get important information, such as passwords.
* Information is often stolen by hacking the network or placing software unknowingly on a computer.

PHISHING

This is an act of sending an email to a user misleading them to believe it is from a trusted person or organisation.
* Phishing — An act of sending an e-mail to a user misleading him to believe that it is from a trusted person or organisation.
* The user is asked to visit a website to update or validate personal details like username, password, and credit card details.
* Social Engineering — Refers to psychologically manipulation of people into performing actions or divulging confidential information.
* Operators of fake websites commit crimes in the victim's name after stealing their identity.
* Vishing — The criminal practice of using social influence over the telephone system, most often using features facilitated by Voice over IP (VoIP), to gain access to sensitive information such as credit card details from the public. The term is a combination of "Voice" and phishing.

PHARMING

In this attack, malicious code redirects users without their knowledge.
* Pharming — In this type of attack, a piece of malicious code is installed on a computer or a server, which misdirects the users to fraudulent websites without their knowledge in order to obtain personal information.
* It aims to obtain passwords or account numbers.
* Unlike phishing, it does not always rely on the user clicking a link; the redirection happens automatically due to the malicious code.

CYBERCRIMES AGAINST ORGANISATION OR SOCIETY

These are planned and politically motivated attacks causing large-scale disorder.

VIRUS

A computer virus disrupts the normal functioning of a computer and destroys data.
* Virus — A computer virus is a program or a set of programs that disrupts the normal functioning of a computer.
* VIRUS — Full form: Vital Information Resources Under Seize.
* Viruses are self-replicating programs that infect more files, resulting in loss of speed and destruction of data.
* Almost all viruses are attached to an executable file; they do not infect unless you run or open the malicious program.
* MyDoom — Considered to be the most expensive virus in the world and in cyber security history. It caused an estimated financial damage of $38.5 billion.
* Cyberespionage — The act or practice of obtaining secrets from individuals, competitors, rivals, groups, governments, and enemies also for military, political, or economic advantage. It is done using illegal exploitation methods on the internet, networks, software, and computers.

Types of Virus

  • Boot Sector Virus — Damages the boot sector of a hard disc or floppy drive. It becomes active at the time of booting the machine and destroys data in the hard disk. Example: Disk killer, Stone virus.
  • Program File Virus — Infects executable files or programs (extensions like .com, .exe, .ovl, .drv, .sys). They get loaded in memory during execution. Example: Sunday, Cascade.
  • Multipartite Virus — A hybrid of a Boot Sector and Program File virus that spreads in multiple ways. It infects the boot sector and executable files. Example: Invader, Flip, Tequila.
  • Polymorphic Virus — Exhibits itself in different forms every time it infects a file and changes its binary pattern to avoid detection. Example: Elkern, Marburg, Satan bug.
  • Network Virus — Rapidly spread through a Local Area Network (LAN) or internet via shared resources. Example: Nimda, SQL Slammer.
  • Macro Virus — Associated with application software like Word, Excel, PowerPoint. It gets loaded into memory when opening an infected document. Example: Melissa.A, Relax, bablas.
  • Clickjacking — A malicious technique of tricking a web user into clicking on something different from what the user perceives he is clicking on.
  • Bots — One of the most sophisticated types of crime-ware applications that perform a wide variety of automated tasks on behalf of the cyber criminals.

CYBERVANDALISM

It involves creating malicious programs to harm computers or editing online content maliciously.
* Cybervandalism — The creation of malicious programs to perform harmful tasks, like physically harming a computer or erasing hard drive data.
* It also involves defaming a website or disrupting businesses by editing online content.

HACKING

Hacking refers to illegal entry into a computer system or network.
* Hacking — Refers to an illegal entry into a computer system or network.
* Hackers write or use ready-made programs to attack target computers.
* Some hack for enjoyment, while others hack for monetary gains like stealing credit card information or extorting money.

CYBERSQUATTING

This involves using someone else's trademark goodwill for benefit.
* Cybersquatting — Refers to the act of using the goodwill of a trademark belonging to someone else and getting benefit out of it.

FORGERY

This is imitating or making false copies of documents.
* Forgery — It is an act of imitating or making a false copy of a document like Birth Certificate, Driving Licence, Currency notes, Cheque Book, Passport, etc., with an intent to cheat an individual or organisation.
* Special software, scanners, and devices are used to recreate these documents for scams.

CYBEREXTORTION

The victim is attacked first and then demanded to pay money.
* Cyberextortion — It is a crime in which the victim is attacked first and then demanded to pay money to stop further attacks.
* It may be done by blocking system resources using Ransomware or Denial of Service (DOS) attack.
* Hacked emails are used to force users to click links that install malware.

CYBERTERRORISM

Used to conduct terrorism through computers to cause fear and panic.
* Cyberterrorism — It is used to conduct terrorism through the use of computers and the internet in order to cause fear and panic.
* It causes large-scale disruption of networks to compel a government or people to fulfil social, religious, or political objectives.
* Attacks on military installations, power plants, air traffic controls, and banks are examples.
* Con Man — The one who exploits characteristics of the human psyche such as greed, honesty and dishonesty, vanity, compassion, credulity, irresponsibility. He tries to get something of value for nothing.

CYBER SECURITY

Cyber security protects networks, computers, programs, and data from attack or unauthorized access.

ANTIVIRUS

Antivirus identifies, prevents, and removes viruses.
* Antivirus — A computer program that is designed to identify, prevent, and remove viruses from a computer.
* Popular software includes McAfee, AVG, Quick Heal, Microsoft Security Essentials, Symantec Norton.
* Full System Scans — Needed when installing antivirus for the first time or after updates to ensure no hidden viruses are present.
* Custom Scan — Helps scan the system as per your requirements.
* Virus Definitions — Antivirus software depends on these to identify malware; regular updates are required for new virus definitions.
* Actions Taken by Antivirus:
* Repair it: The identified virus gets removed from the infected file.
* Delete it: The infected file gets deleted along with the virus.
* Quarantine it: When unsure if a file is infected, the program separates the file so the virus does not spread.

FIREWALLS

Firewalls prevent unauthorized users from accessing the computer through the network.
* Firewall — A security system that protects your computer by preventing unauthorised users from gaining access to it through network or internet.
* It can be hardware, software, or a combination.
* It is implemented on the gateway of a network and follows rules defined by the user or administrator to control incoming and outgoing traffic.

ENCRYPTION SOFTWARE

Encryption protects data privacy during transmission.
* Encryption — The process of converting your electronic data into a form that cannot be easily understood by anyone.
* Cipher text — The encrypted text produced by encryption software.
* Decryption — The process of reading an encrypted message, which has to be decrypted with the same software on the receiver's side.
* Software applications like GnuPG or GPG can be used to encrypt data files and emails.

BIOMETRICS

This is the safest method to give authorization rights based on unique characteristics.
* Biometrics — It is the safest method to give authorisation rights to an individual. In Biometrics, a person is verified by means of his/her unique biological or physical characteristics.
* Identification methods include retinal scan, fingerprints, and voice recognition.
* Characteristics are saved in a database and matched when a person tries to access the system.

PASSWORDS

Passwords guarantee that the system is accessed only by authorized users.
* Passwords — A password is a sequence of characters, numbers and special symbols that a user is supposed to enter to gain access to the computer.
* Strong passwords should be used to protect data and important information.

BACKUPS

Backups allow restoration of data in case of loss.
* Backups — In computer terminology, backup is the process of making duplicate copies of data, which can be used to restore the original data in case of any loss of it.
* Backups can restore files deleted accidentally or corrupted by a virus.

COOKIES

Cookies store user-specific data for customized webpages.
* Cookies — A cookie is a text message given to a web browser by a web server. The web browser saves this user-specific data in a text file.
* This file is used by the browser to prepare customised webpages so users do not have to fill stored information every time.
* Cookies are safe documents but can produce harmful consequences if read by an unauthorized person.
* Hackers can use cookies to gain access to sensitive information. It is advised to delete cookies periodically, especially on public computers.

Computer and the internet have become inseparable parts of your life in multiple spheres these days. The booming technologies of the modern age have made your life much easier and more connected across the globe via the interconnected networks. Now-a-days, you are connected to the internet by many means, i.e., through computer, smartphone, or tablet. Although networks of today have simplified the communication process and provided great opportunities in almost all spheres, there are various types of challenges and threats associated with the use of these networks of today. These threats are known as cybercrimes.

CYBERCRIME

A cybercrime is any illegal activity done through the internet e.g., identity theft; where somebody can steal your e-mail id or password and use it to send fake e-mails to people containing false information about the product or winning a lottery, etc.

Then there are credit card account thefts, internet frauds like, ordering goods in your name, extracting mobile phone contacts, etc., forgery, i.e., imitating documents and currency, harassing others and mischief mongering, by sending threatening messages, all of which come under the jurisdiction of the Indian Penal Code (IPC).

Cybercrimes can be divided into three main categories:
* Crime against an individual person
* Crime against property
* Crime against an organisation/society

VIRUS

A computer virus is a program or a set of programs that disrupts the normal functioning of a computer. A virus infects or destroys data. Like biological virus, it enters into the host (computer) without the permission or knowledge of the host. The full form of VIRUS is Vital Information Resources Under Seize. The essential resources of a computer, like memory, processing speed, data, and programs are hampered when a virus enters into the system. Virus programs are self-replicating programs, which continuously expand their reach by infecting more and more computer files, resulting in the loss of speed and destruction of data. Creating a computer virus and spreading it is a cybercrime. Almost all the viruses are attached to an executable file, which means that if a virus exists on a computer, it will not infect the computer unless you run or open the malicious program.

Types of Virus

There are different types of viruses but broadly they can be classified into the following categories:

Boot Sector Virus

This type of virus damages the boot sector of a hard disc or floppy drive. A disk or hard drive comprises smaller sections known as Sectors. The first sector is called the boot sector. It holds the master boot record, which is responsible for reading and loading the operating system. Boot Sector Virus becomes active at the time of booting the machine and destroys the data in hard disk.

For example: Disk killer, Stone virus, etc.

[IMAGE_START] Illustration of a laptop computer. On the screen, a magnifying glass is shown inspecting a yellow biohazard symbol inside a circle. [IMAGE_END]

Program File Virus

This virus infects the executable files or programs, such as those with extension, like .com, .exe, .ovl, .drv, .sys, etc. These programs get loaded in the memory during the execution. The virus becomes active in the memory by replicating itself and infecting files and other programs on the disk with the intention of causing permanent damage or making them unreadable.

For example: Sunday, Cascade, etc.

Multipartite Virus

A multipartite virus is a hybrid of a Boot Sector and Program File virus that spreads in multiple ways. The virus gets entry into the computer system through some infected media and then resides inside the computer memory. After that this virus moves into the hard drive, infect, the boot sector and starts spreading in the system by infecting the executable files.

[IMAGE_START] Small illustration of a laptop computer. The screen displays a red background with a white skull and crossbones symbol, representing a virus infection. [IMAGE_END]

Know the Fact

MyDoom is considered to be the most expensive virus in the world and in cyber security history. It caused an estimated financial damage of $38.5 billion.

For example: Invader, Flip, Tequila, etc.

Polymorphic Virus

As the name suggests (poly-many, morph-forms), this type of virus exhibits itself in different forms every time it infects a file and changes its binary pattern to avoid detection by an antivirus program. For examples: Elkern, Marburg, Satan bug, etc.

Network Virus

Network viruses rapidly spread through a Local Network Area (LAN), and sometimes throughout the internet. Generally, network viruses multiply through shared resources, i.e., shared drives and folders. When the virus infects a computer, it searches through the network to attack its new potential prey. When the virus finishes infecting that computer, it moves on to the next and the cycle repeats itself. The most dangerous network viruses are Nimda and SQL Slammer.

ANTIVIRUS

Antivirus is a computer program that is designed to identify, prevent, and remove viruses from a computer. It performs the following tasks in a computer:

  • Scans the computer files to look for known viruses from virus dictionary.
  • Identifies suspicious behaviour of any computer program, which might indicate infection.
  • Scans incoming e-mails, which might contain virus in the attachment.

Some popular antivirus software in use nowadays, are as follows:
McAfee, AVG Antivirus, Quick Heal, Microsoft Security Essentials, Symantec Norton Antivirus.
