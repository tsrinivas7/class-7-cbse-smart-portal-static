# ALGEBRAIC EXPRESSIONS

A combination of constants (1, 2, 3, ...) and variables (a, m, l, p, ...) connected by the signs of fundamental mathematical operations (+, -, ├Ś or ├Ę) is called an algebraic expression. For example, 4m + 7, 3y - 8, $\frac{x}{2}$ - 3, etc. are all algebraic expressions. Algebraic expressions can be formed by different combinations. Let us discuss these combinations with suitable examples.

1. Combining variables with themselves: The algebraic expression $x^2$ is formed by multiplying the variable $x$ with itself, $x \times x = x^2$.
2. Combining variables with other variables: The algebraic expression $(x + y)$ is formed by adding two variables $x$ and $y$, whereas the algebraic expression $xyz$ is formed by just multiplying the three variables $x$, $y$ and $z$.
3. Combining constants and variables: The algebraic expression $2yz$ is formed by multiplying the constant 2 with the product of the variables $y$ and $z$.

## Terms of an Algebraic Expression

Terms are added to form algebraic expressions. For example, in the algebraic expression $3x - 5$, $3x$ and $-5$ are the two terms. Similarly, $5p^2$, $4pq$ and $-6$ are the three terms in the algebraic expression $5p^2 + 4pq - 6$.

A term of an algebraic expression could be:
- a constant, for example, 2, -33, 45, etc.
- a variable, for example, $a$, $b$, $x$, $y$, etc.
- product of a constant and variables, for example, $3x$, $\frac{1}{2}ab$, etc.
- product of variables, for example, $xy$, $x^3y^5$, etc.

## Grasp It

Always include ŌĆś-ŌĆÖ sign with the negative term.

## Factors of a Term

Consider the expression $5x^2 - 4xy$. This expression consists of two terms, $5x^2$ and $-4xy$. The term $5x^2$ is the product of 5, $x$ and $x$. We say that, 5, $x$ and $x$ are the factors of the term $5x^2$. A term is a product of its factors. The term $-4xy$ is a product of the factors $-4$, $x$ and $y$. The terms and factors of an expression can be conveniently represented by a tree diagram. The tree for the expression $(5x^2 - 4xy)$ is shown in Fig. 6.7.

## Coefficients

We have already learnt to write a term of an algebraic expression as the product of its factors. Factors may be variables or constants. A term generally consists of one numerical factor (or constant), the rest being variables, that is, algebraic factors. The numerical factor of a term is said to be the numerical coefficient or simply the coefficient of a term. For example, in $7ab^2$, 7 is the coefficient of the term. Here, $a$, $b$ and $b$ are called algebraic factors.

<!-- Page: 81 -->

However, coefficients need not only be numerical. Coefficient is a relative term.
In a term, any one of the factors is called the coefficient of the product of the other factors.
For example, in \(7ab^2\), 7 is the coefficient of \(ab^2\), \(a\) is the coefficient of \(7b^2\) and \(b^2\) is the coefficient of \(7a\).

## Grasp It
When the coefficient of a term is +1, it is usually omitted while writing the term.
For example, \(1x^2y\) is written simply as \(x^2y\), \(1xyz\) is written as \(xyz\) and so on. When the coefficient is \((-1)\), it is represented only by the minus sign. Thus, \((-1)x^2y\) is written as \(-x^2y\) and \((-1)xyz\) is written as \(-xyz\).

## Types of Algebraic Expressions
Algebraic expressions can be classified on the basis of the number of terms they have.
- **Monomial**: An algebraic expression which contains only one term is called a monomial. For example, \(5m\), \(6pq\), \(8xyz\), \(-7\), etc. are all monomials.
- **Binomial**: An algebraic expression which contains two unlike terms is called a binomial. For example, \(5a + 3b\), \(5x - 6y\), \(2pq + 5qr\), etc. are all binomials. The expression \(2t + 6t\) is not a binomial as the terms \(2t\) and \(6t\) are like terms.
- **Trinomial**: An algebraic expression which contains three unlike terms is called a trinomial. For example, \(a + b + c\), \(p + 2q + 3r\), \(3m - 2n + 4p\), etc. are all trinomials. The expression \(a + 6b - 2a\) is not a trinomial as the terms \(a\) and \(-2a\) are like terms.
- **Polynomial**: An algebraic expression with one or more terms is called a polynomial. Thus, a monomial, a binomial and a trinomial are all polynomials. For example, \(x^2 + y - 4z + 1\) is a polynomial.

### Get It
- \(4x^2 + 3x + 6\) is a polynomial. \(\checkmark\)
- \(4x^2 + \frac{3}{x} + 6\) is a polynomial. \(\times\) (None of the terms can have a negative exponent for any variable.)

## Like and Unlike Terms
The terms with same algebraic factors are called like terms. In like terms, the numerical coefficient may be different. For example, \(5xy\) and \(3xy\) are like terms. On the other hand, the terms with different algebraic factors are called unlike terms. For example, \(2x^2y\) and \(-2xy^2\) are unlike terms, as the algebraic factors of the two terms are different.

## Degree of a Polynomial
The highest power of a variable in a polynomial is called its degree. For example, in the polynomial \(a^2 + 6a + 4\), the highest power of the variable \(a\) is 2. Thus, the degree of this polynomial is 2.
The degree of a polynomial is decided by the term with the higher power. The degree of a term containing more than one variable is obtained by

adding the powers of all the variables in a term.
For example, the polynomial $-23x^2y + 11xy - 8$ is of degree 3 as the highest power of its term ($x^2y$) is 3. Similarly, the degree of the polynomial $a^5b^2 + a^2b^2 + a^3b + ab$ is 7.

### Grasp It
- The degree of a constant term is 0.
- Writing a polynomial in decreasing order of the powers of its terms is called its standard form.

#### Solved Example
**Example 4:** Write the degree of each of the following polynomials.
a) $a + a^2 + a^4 + 2$
b) $1 + x^3 + x^5 - x^2$

#### Solution:
a) Arranging the terms of the given polynomial in descending order of their powers, we get:
\[ a^4 + a^2 + a + 2 \]
Thus, the degree of this polynomial is 4.
b) Arranging the terms of the given polynomial in descending order of their powers, we get:
\[ x^5 + x^3 - x^2 + 1 \]
Thus, the degree of this polynomial is 5.
c) Arranging the terms of the given polynomial in descending order of their powers, we get:
\[ x^5y^3 - x^4y^2 + x^3y - x^2 + xy \]
Thus, the degree of this polynomial is 8.

**ADDITION OF ALGEBRAIC
EXPRESSIONS**

Like numbers, we can also add algebraic
expressions.
To add two or more like terms, we add
the numerical coefficients of all the
like terms. For example, let us add $23x$, $-17x$
and $-99x$.
$23x + (-17x) + (-99x) = [23 + (-17) + (-99)]x$
$= -93x$

**Grasp It**
- The sum of two or more like terms is a
like term with a numerical coefficient
equal to the sum of the numerical
coefficients of all the like terms.
- Unlike terms cannot be added.

Algebraic expressions which include like and
unlike terms can be added by collecting different
groups of like terms and adding them.

## Subtraction of Algebraic Expressions

Like addition, we can subtract only like terms. Subtracting a term from another term is same as adding its additive inverse to the other term. For example, subtracting \(3x\) from \(5x\) is same as adding \(-3x\) to \(5x\).

### Grasp It
- The difference of two like terms is a like term with a numerical coefficient equal to the difference of the numerical coefficients of the two like terms.
- Unlike terms cannot be subtracted.

## Solved Examples

Example 9: Subtract \(22p\) from \(23p\).
Solution: \(23p - 22p = (23 - 22)p = p\)

Example 10: Subtract \((-23a)\) from \(377a\).

# FINDING THE VALUE OF AN ALGEBRAIC EXPRESSION

We know that an algebraic expression has variables and a variable can take any value. Thus, the value of an algebraic expression depends on the values of the variables forming the expression. We can find the value of an algebraic expression by replacing the variables with numbers.

## Solved Examples

### Example 15: Find the value of the expression $17x + 19x^2 + 21x^3$, when $x = 2$.

**Solution:** When $x = 2$, $17x + 19x^2 + 21x^3 = 17 \times 2 + 19 \times 2^2 + 21 \times 2^3 = 17 \times 2 + 19 \times 4 + 21 \times 8 = 34 + 76 + 168 = 278$

### Example 16: Simplify the following algebraic expression and find its value, when $x = -4$ and $y = 3$.

$(x^2 + xy - y^2) + 3 - (2xy + 3x^2)$

**Solution:** Simplifying the algebraic expression by combining like terms, we get $(x^2 + xy - y^2) + 3 - 2xy - 3x^2 = (x^2 - 3x^2) + (xy - 2xy) - y^2 + 3 = -2x^2 - xy - y^2 + 3$

### Substituting $x = -4$ and $y = 3$, we get

$= -2 \times (-4)^2 - (-4) \times 3 - 3^2 + 3$

$= -2 \times 16 - (-12) - 9 + 3$

$= -32 + 12 - 9 + 3$

$= -41 + 15$

## USING ALGEBRAIC EXPRESSIONS

We already know the formula of area of a rectangle which is given by

Area = length ├Ś breadth

This formula is written as: Area = $l \times b$

This formula can be used to find the area of a rectangle of any size.

One more practical example of this kind of formula is that of simple interest.

S.I. = $\frac{P \times R \times T}{100}$, where

$P$ is the principal amount, $R$ is the rate of interest and $T$ is the time period. For any values of $P$, $R$ and $T$, simple interest can be found using this formula.

## Wrap Up

- An algebraic expression is formed when variables and constants are combined using one or more fundamental operations.
- An algebraic expression is made up of terms. Terms are added to make an expression.
- A term is the product of its factor. Factors containing variables are called algebraic factors.
- The coefficient of a term is the numerical factor of the term.
- Terms with the same algebraic factors are called like terms, while terms with different algebraic factors are called unlike terms.
- Any algebraic expression having one or more terms with variables whose powers are positive integers is called a polynomial. Specifically, a one-term expression is a monomial, a two-term expression is a binomial and a three-term expression is a trinomial.

## Mental Maths

### Understand

- If any variable in an algebraic expression appears in the denominator, then the algebraic expression is not a polynomial. All polynomials are algebraic expressions but all algebraic expressions are not polynomials.
- Terms containing the same algebraic factors are called like terms, whereas the terms containing different algebraic factors are called unlike terms.
- The value of an algebraic expression depends on the values of the variables involved in it.
