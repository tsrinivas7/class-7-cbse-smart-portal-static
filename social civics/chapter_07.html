Chapter 7: Understanding Media and Advertising

📢 What is Media?

Media refers to any means of communicating information to people.
Mass media reaches a large number of people at once.

Types of Mass Media:

  • Television (cable, satellite)
  • Radio
  • Cinema (movies)
  • Print media (newspapers, magazines, journals, books)
  • Internet (websites, social media)

💡 New forms of media, like social media and online platforms, have changed how we get information and interact with content.

🌐 Role of Media in Democracy

In a democracy, the media plays a key role by:
- Informing the public about important issues
- Helping people form public opinion
- Holding the government accountable
- Giving a platform for public debate

📌 Public opinion is the total of individual beliefs and attitudes held by adults.
In a democracy, the government must listen to and act on public opinion.

🔍 Right to Information (RTI) Act, 2005

  • Any Indian citizen can request information from public authorities.
  • The RTI Act came into force on 12 October 2005 (now includes Jammu & Kashmir too).
  • Citizens can access records, documents, emails, reports, and more.

✅ Why it matters:
It helps people stay informed and hold government officials accountable.

📣 What is Advertising?

Advertising is a way to promote products or services to increase sales and create awareness.

Two Main Types of Advertising:

Type Purpose Example
Commercial Advertising Promotes products to sell more "Buy this phone – 75% off!"
Social Advertising Raises awareness about social issues "Say No to Child Labour"

🚨 Some ads make false claims. The government and media now encourage honesty in ads.

Why Do Companies Advertise?

  • To increase sales
  • To build a strong brand identity
  • To inform people about new products
  • To influence public opinion

📱 Social media advertising is very effective today because it reaches many people directly.

💡 What is Consumerism?

Consumerism is the strong desire to buy material goods (like clothes, phones, cars).
- People now value luxury and comfort over inner peace or values.
- This has changed how we live and think — we often focus on what we own rather than what we value.

⚠️ Too much consumerism can lead to waste, stress, and loss of focus on family, nature, and personal growth.

📚 Media and Ethics

Media should follow these basic rules:
1. Report only true and accurate information.
2. Stay independent and fair (not biased).
3. Avoid causing harm to society.

A responsible media helps build trust and supports democracy.

🏛️ Media and Democracy – Key Points

  • Communication is essential for social welfare and human dignity.
  • A democratic society needs free and fair media to allow public discussion.
  • Media acts as a public arena where citizens can express views and influence decisions.
  • Today, TV and online platforms are major sources of political news.

📣 The media helps turn private citizens into a public body through public opinion.

3. State whether the following statements are 'True' or 'False'

(a) Electronic media consists of TV, radio and entertainment.
❌ False
Explanation: Electronic media includes TV, radio, film, internet, and digital platforms. Entertainment is a function, not a component of electronic media.

(b) The information provided by the media should be authentic.
✅ True
Explanation: Authenticity is a basic principle of media ethics. False or misleading information undermines public trust.

(c) The new technologies help media to reach more people.
✅ True
Explanation: Internet, social media, and mobile platforms allow media to reach wider and more diverse audiences.

(d) An independent media is free from influence and control.
✅ True
Explanation: Independent media operates without bias or government interference, ensuring fair and balanced reporting.

(e) Brand building is an important function of brand franchise.
❌ False
Explanation: Brand franchise refers to the ability of a brand to grow without continuous advertising. Brand building is a separate function of advertising, not of brand franchise.

5. Very Short Answer Type Questions

(a) What do you understand by the term 'media'?
Media refers to any means used to convey information to a large number of people. Examples include television, radio, newspapers, internet, and films.

(b) What are the two types of media?
The two main types of media are:
1. Print media (e.g., newspapers, magazines, books)
2. Electronic media (e.g., television, radio, internet, films)

(c) What are advertisements?
Advertisements are messages or promotions designed to inform people about the availability, quality, and benefits of a product or service, with the aim of encouraging purchase.

(d) What is the purpose of advertisements?
The purpose of advertisements is to increase sales, build brand awareness, create consumer interest, and promote new products or services.

(e) What is commercial advertising?
Commercial advertising is designed to promote the sale of a product or service by highlighting its features, benefits, and quality to influence consumer demand.

6. Short Answer Type Questions

(a) What impact does changing technology have on media and the minds of people?
Changing technology has transformed media by making it faster, more accessible, and interactive. People now consume news and information instantly through smartphones and social media. This has led to shorter attention spans, increased misinformation, and a shift from passive consumption to active participation. It has also influenced people’s values, making them more materialistic and dependent on instant gratification.

(b) What do you mean by public opinion?
Public opinion is the collective set of beliefs, attitudes, and views held by the adult population on various issues. It reflects how the public perceives political, social, or economic matters and influences government decisions in a democracy.

(c) Why is independent media a basic need for a country?
Independent media is essential because it ensures transparency, accountability, and freedom of expression. It acts as a watchdog, exposing corruption, holding leaders responsible, and informing the public about important issues. Without independent media, the government can manipulate information, leading to poor decision-making and loss of public trust.

(d) Enlist the basic principles of media ethics.
The basic principles of media ethics are:
1. Provide only true and factual reports.
2. Be independent and avoid bias.
3. Minimise harm to individuals and society.
4. Respect privacy and dignity of people.
5. Avoid sensationalism and misinformation.

(e) Why do we mostly see distinguished public figures or personalities in social advertisements?
Public figures are often used in social advertisements because they have credibility, trust, and influence. Their presence adds authority and authenticity to the message. People are more likely to believe and act upon messages when they are associated with respected individuals, especially in issues like health, education, or child labour.

7. Long Answer Type Questions

(a) What role does media play in a democracy? Discuss.
Media plays a crucial role in a democracy by acting as a bridge between the people and the government. It informs citizens about political developments, policies, and public issues. It helps shape public opinion, encourages debate, and holds leaders accountable. Media provides a platform for diverse voices, promotes transparency, and ensures that decisions are made in the public interest. In a democracy, media enables citizens to participate in decision-making through informed voting and public discourse. It also helps in promoting social values like justice, equality, and freedom. Without a free and independent media, democracy would lack transparency and public awareness.

(b) 'Advertising is more than selling things'. Explain.
Advertising is indeed more than just selling products. It educates the public about new technologies, health issues, environmental concerns, and social values. For example, advertisements on water conservation, child labour, or vaccination raise awareness and promote positive social change. They build brand identity, create trust, and influence consumer behaviour. Social advertisements, in particular, aim to change attitudes and encourage responsible actions. Thus, advertising serves as a tool for public education and social transformation, not just commercial gain.

(c) We see a number of advertisements every day. We get driven away and buy the products. Is it good for us? Should we not judge its importance to us? Think and answer.
Yes, advertisements can be beneficial, but they should be judged critically. While they help us discover new products and services, excessive or misleading ads can create unnecessary desires, especially in children. Some ads make false claims or promote materialism, which may harm mental and emotional well-being. We should not blindly follow ads but evaluate their content—checking if the product is truly needed, if the claims are accurate, and if the message promotes responsibility or harm. Critical thinking helps us make informed choices and avoid being manipulated by commercial interests.

(d) By what means can media infuse ethical values among the people in general? Describe.
Media can infuse ethical values through:
- Social advertisements on issues like honesty, respect, gender equality, and environmental care.
- Reporting on real-life stories of moral courage, justice, and community service.
- Promoting public debates on ethical issues such as corruption, human rights, and social justice.
- Using role models (e.g., teachers, doctors, activists) to demonstrate responsible behaviour.
- Educational content on values like responsibility, empathy, and integrity.
By consistently presenting ethical narratives, media helps shape a society that values truth, fairness, and mutual respect.*

(e) Are you on Facebook? Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of Facebook as a social networking site.
Yes, Facebook is one of the most widely used social networking platforms.

Advantages:
- Connects people across the world.
- Enables sharing of photos, videos, and updates.
- Helps in maintaining relationships with family and friends.
- Provides a platform for groups and discussions on social issues.
- Useful for businesses and education through online presence.

Disadvantages:
- Can spread false information and rumours.
- Encourages cyberbullying and online harassment.
- May lead to addiction and reduced face-to-face interaction.
- Privacy concerns and misuse of personal data.
- Can promote negative emotions and social comparison.

Therefore, while Facebook offers many benefits, it must be used responsibly with awareness of its risks.
